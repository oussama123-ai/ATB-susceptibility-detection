Wath is Antibiotics ?

We’re Always Here to Protect You From Virus

Abstract:

The rapid emergence and spread of antibiotic-resistant bacteria have become a major threat to public health. In clinics, antibiotic susceptibility testing (AST) is employed to guide appropriate prescription to suppress the aggravation of antibiotic resistance. However, the conventional culture-based AST methods are time-consuming and labor-intensive. Therefore, rapid AST approaches are urgently needed. In this review, conventional, commercial, and recent progress in novel imaging-based rapid AST modalities are reviewed with the merits and limitations of each recent imaging-based method highlighted. Moreover, we also discuss the future directions of applying imaging-based rapid AST to point-of-care diagnosis.

Antibiogram ?

What results can be expected from an antibiogram?

The purpose of the antibiogram is to find an antibiotic that will be effective against a given infection.the antibiogram makes it possible to distinguish between:

  • a strain of resistant bacteria: the antibiotic tested cannot be effective in the patient
  • a strain sensitive to such or such antibiotic: it is this treatment that will be prescribed.
When your doctor prescribes antibiotic treatment, it is crucial to respect the doses and the duration of the treatment, even if the symptoms disappear after a few days. Stopping treatment too early promotes the emergence of bacteria that are resistant to antibiotics and therefore difficult to treat.


To carry out the antibiogram, it is necessary to obtain a bacterial sample from the patient. Depending on the site of infection (pulmonary, blood, urine, etc.), the sample will be taken in different ways and will be sent to the analysis laboratory. There are several techniques for carrying out an antibiogram. Most often, the bacteria are "seeded" on the surface of a Petri dish, on a gelled medium, and discs impregnated with a known dose of different antibiotics are placed on the surface of the agar. If the antibiotic is ineffective, the bacteria can still grow and we can measure the size of their colony. On the contrary, if the antibiotic is effective, we will see on the surface of the disc “zones of inhibition”, where bacterial growth has been inhibited.The result is usually obtained in 24 hours.
